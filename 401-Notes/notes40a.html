Class 34: Ethics

June 22, 2023

Ethics in the workplace

  • The article "The code I’m still ashamed of" raises important ethical concerns in the workplace regarding the impact of decisions made by software developers. It highlights the author's experience of working on code that had unintended negative consequences. This article underscores the responsibility of developers to consider the ethical implications of their work and the potential harm it can cause.
  • "Project Dragonfly, Google’s censored search engine" discusses the ethical dilemma faced by Google in creating a censored search engine for the Chinese market. It brings to light the tension between business interests and human rights, raising questions about the moral responsibility of technology companies to respect freedom of expression and user privacy.
  • The article "Amazon workers demand Jeff Bezos cancel 'Recognition' software" sheds light on the ethical concerns surrounding Amazon's facial recognition software. It highlights the potential for misuse and abuse of such technology, leading to concerns about invasion of privacy and the potential for discriminatory practices.

Ethics in Technology

  • The topic of self-driving car ethics is explored in the article "Self Driving Car Ethics" which discusses the ethical considerations involved in programming autonomous vehicles. It raises questions about how these vehicles should prioritize the safety of passengers, pedestrians, and other drivers, and the moral implications of decisions made by the AI systems.
  • "Big Data is our Civil Rights issue" highlights the ethical challenges posed by the collection and use of large amounts of data. It draws attention to the potential for discrimination and violations of privacy rights, emphasizing the need for ethical guidelines and regulations to protect individuals' civil rights in the era of big data and AI.
  • The article "Will democracy survive big data and AI?" raises concerns about the impact of technology on democratic processes. It explores how the use of big data and AI algorithms can influence elections and shape public opinion, calling for transparency and accountability in the use of these technologies to safeguard democratic values.

Tech Company Principles

  • Microsoft AI Principles outlines Microsoft's ethical commitments in the development and deployment of AI technologies. It emphasizes principles such as fairness, reliability, privacy, and transparency, reflecting the company's effort to address ethical concerns and promote responsible AI practices.
  • The Ethical OS Toolkit provides a framework for addressing ethical challenges in technology development. It offers tools and resources to identify potential ethical risks and mitigate their impact, fostering a culture of ethics and responsible decision-making within tech companies.
  • Google AI Principles presents Google's guiding principles for AI development and usage. It emphasizes the importance of developing AI for the benefit of society, ensuring fairness, and avoiding harmful applications. The principles emphasize transparency, accountability, and the responsible handling of data.

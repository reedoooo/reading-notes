Implementation: Stack and Queue Visualization

  1. Stack Visualization:
    • Imagine a stack of books placed one on top of another. Each book represents an element in the stack.
    • The stack follows the Last-In-First-Out (LIFO) principle.
    • Demonstrate pushing (adding) and popping (removing) books from the stack.
    • Emphasize that you can only access the top book of the stack.
  2. Queue Visualization:
    • Picture a line of people waiting for a movie ticket. Each person represents an element in the queue.
    • The queue follows the First-In-First-Out (FIFO) principle.
    • Show how new people join the line at the end (enqueue) and how the person at the front leaves the line (dequeue).
    • Explain that you can only access the person at the front of the queue.

Extracting State Logic into a Reducer

  • Motivation for adding a reducer: To manage more complex state logic that involves multiple sub-values or when the next state depends on the previous one.
  • Actions in the context of a reducer: Actions are objects that contain information about what happened and what needs to change. They're different from setting state directly because they describe a user's action, not the new state.
  • Common list operation that useReduce is named for: Reducing a collection of values down to a single value.
  • Switching from useState to useReducer: When state logic becomes complex due to multiple sub-values, or when the next state depends on the previous one, it's good to switch to useReducer.

Reflection

  • Learning goals after reading and reviewing the class README: Understanding the concept of reducers and how they can be used to manage complex state in React applications.

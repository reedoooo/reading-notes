Reading: 5 steps to RBAC

  • Role Based Access Control (RBAC) is a security model that provides access to resources based on roles and permissions.
  • In a Role/Permission hierarchy implemented using RBAC, there could be different roles such as "Admin," "Manager," and "User."
  • To implement RBAC, one approach would be to first identify the roles required in the system and define the permissions associated with each role.
  • Additional Resources: wiki - RBAC

Videos: RBAC tutorial

  • Access rights associated with RBAC are primarily associated with the role rather than the individual user.
  • Access rights, or authorization, are activated after a user successfully authenticates their identity.
  • RBAC can benefit a business in several ways: enhanced security, simplified administration, and improved compliance.

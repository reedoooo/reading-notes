June 23, 2023

Article 1: First Timers Only

  • Website dedicated to helping newcomers get involved in open source projects.
  • Provides various resources to assist newcomers, including websites that aggregate projects looking for help, guides on how to contribute to open source projects, and a bot that tweets when a new first-timers-only issue is posted.
  • Encourages project owners to label certain issues as 'first-timers-only', demonstrating their willingness to guide newcomers through their first PR.
  • The 'first-timers-only' label is significant as it signals to newcomers that they are welcome and valued, making it easier for them to make their first contribution.
  • First Timers Only was proposed by Kent C. Dodds to encourage new people to make their first contribution. Scott Hanselman and others also supported the idea, emphasizing kindness in open source contributions.
  • There are Twitter accounts and bots, such as @yourfirstpr and @first_tmrs_only, which highlight great issues that a newcomer can solve to make their first PR.
  • There's also a GitHub app named First Timers that automates most of the process of creating 'first-timers-only' issues.
  • First Timers Only is all about empowering newcomers to start contributing to open source projects, emphasizing that everyone starts somewhere.

Article 2: Open Source Guide

  • Open a pull request for trivial fixes or for work that's been discussed in an issue.
  • Pull requests can be opened as drafts or marked as Work in Progress (WIP).
  • The submission process involves forking and cloning the repository, creating a branch for edits, referencing any relevant issues, testing changes, and contributing in the style of the project.
  • After submission, contributions may not get a response, may require changes, may not be accepted, or may be accepted.
  • It's important to be responsive, respectful, and understanding throughout the contribution process.
  • Regardless of the outcome, contributors should be grateful for the effort put in by maintainers and remember that open source is made possible by people like them.

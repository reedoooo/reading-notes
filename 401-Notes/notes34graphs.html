What is a Graph?

  • A graph is a non-linear data structure that represents a pictorial structure of a set of objects where some pairs of objects are connected by links.
  • The interconnected objects are represented by points termed as vertices, and the links that connect the vertices are called edges.
  • The vertices may also have additional information called 'weights' associated with them.
  • Graphs are used to represent networks, which include computer networks, social networks, biological networks, etc.

Key Concepts in Graphs

  • A vertex (or node) is the fundamental unit of which graphs are formed. In a diagram of a graph, a vertex is usually represented as a dot with a label.
  • An edge (or arc) is an unordered pair of vertices that are considered adjacent. Edges may be directed or undirected.
  • A path in a graph is a sequence of edges connecting two vertices.
  • A cycle in a directed graph is a path that starts and ends at the same vertex.
  • A graph without cycles is called an acyclic graph. A directed graph without cycles is called a directed acyclic graph or DAG.
